LUMI

Interactive system

Scroll down

The aim was to design an interactive system including a smart object for children, a website and a mobile application. This project was developed in a team as part of the interaction design course. Its main concept was turning an annoying task for children into something fun.

Its final outcome was meant to be something engaging and that, indirectly, forced children to get out of the bed in a playful way. At the same time, helping parents deal with mornings in an easy way.


Research

There were three research methods applied. At first, the investigation was fully digital, through web platforms. As the project advanced, we resorted to personal interviews with children and their parents. As a last source, we created an online survey directed once more to children and their parents, to understand their relationship.

During the research, we discovered that kids and parents were struggling with the morning routine. There were common activities that children didn’t want or didn’t know how to accomplish and parents didn’t know how to encourage them.

Personas’ Design

After analysing the outcome of the research, we were able to understand the requirements of our interaction system. In order to create the personas, we focused on some meaningful characteristics.

Filippo & Marina


messy / dependent - messy / relaxed


Filippo isn’t autonomous and when he tries to do something he ends up in a mess. Marina, his mom, does her best to manage the morning routine, but she’s always in a hurry and the results are usually negative.

Paola & Alessandro


methodical / dependent - overparenting / messy


Paola is extremely sensitive and she gets really frustrated when she fails. Alessandro, her father, finds it difficult to organize his job and housework in an efficient way, as he cares too much about his children.

System design

Our final goal intends to change the perception of waking up children in the morning, helping families to face it in a comfortable way by creating good habits, and breaking the myth that waking up is a hard thing to do.

Website

Designed for parents as its main user, and acts as the first touchpoint of the customer with the system. Its main purpose is to sell the product and raise awareness of its benefits.

Mobile app
demo

Designed for parents as its main user, the application was thought to be able to pair with the smart object, since it is the only way to set it. The principal settings are programming the wake-up time, night light and sound.

Smart object

The physical product was designed for kids. This is the core interaction of the whole system. Its purpose is to achieve the task of waking up children and creating a special bond between children and parents.

The package contains Lumi(the puppet), the spaceship(charger), the Book with Lumi’s story and the Instructions manual.


Lumi's story


Smart object’s interaction flow

The interaction flow of the smart object is divided in two moments. One that takes place when the parents are reading the story to the child, and the other during the waking up time.

Story interaction


The interaction starts when the parents read Lumi’s story book to the child. While reading the book the child can experience three kinds of interaction according to what is happening to Lumi in the story. These interactions are highlighted in the book. The action finishes when the book is over and the kid goes to sleep. Before doing so, he places the puppet on the Spaceship.

Morning interaction


Once the kid has fallen asleep, parents hide Lumi in his bedroom. At the time set by the parents, Lumi starts to produce smooth sounds and glow in order to wake up the child. Lumi’s volume will rise progressively in order to wake up the child. The colour will change according to the time span that corresponds to “Lumi’s feelings”. As soon as the child wakes up, he searches for Lumi. The experience ends when the child is happily awakened and he places Lumi on his Spaceship.
